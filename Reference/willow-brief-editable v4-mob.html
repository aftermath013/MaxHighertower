What is Willow?

The Product Landscape

Willow is a San Francisco-based company that invented the first fully in-bra wearable breast pump in 2017. They are the only major wearable pump brand in the US that is founded, headquartered, and built by women. Their product line spans electric wearable pumps, manual pumps, milk storage bags, and a companion app with SmartStash AI tracking.

Target customer is the working, mobile, multitasking mom — primarily US-based, typically postpartum within the first 12 months. Their flagship Willow Go retails at $299–$329 and is eligible for HSA/FSA spending and partial insurance reimbursement under the Affordable Care Act.

Why This Account Matters

  • High-emotion customer base — postpartum, sleep-deprived, time-sensitive
  • Premium product at $300+ means expectation of premium support
  • Complex contact drivers: technical, billing, insurance, and clinical-adjacent
  • Brand is openly struggling with customer satisfaction (3.5/5 Trustpilot, 80+ BBB complaints)
  • This is a white space opportunity — Willow needs better CX infrastructure

What Customers Are Saying

HIGH

Agent Abandonment Mid-Contact

Multiple BBB complaints document agents disconnecting from live chats mid-resolution, forcing customers to restart the entire interaction from zero. Customers also report being put on hold and never reconnected. This is the single highest-damage touchpoint — it happens during an already stressful postpartum period.

CSAT Killer Escalation Driver Retention Risk
Source: BBB Complaint Archive — Willow Pump, Mountain View CA · bbb.org
HIGH

Suction Failure & Defective Units

A recurring complaint across BBB, Trustpilot, and Walmart reviews: pumps fail to generate suction out of the box, or lose suction within weeks. Customers who paid $300–$500 are left without a functioning pump — which directly impacts their ability to breastfeed. One customer explicitly stated this destroyed her breastfeeding journey for her baby with severe reflux. The emotional stakes are extremely high.

Warranty Trigger Product Failure Emotional Distress
Source: BBB Reviews · Walmart Product Reviews (Willow 3.0 Flextubes, 2024–2025)
HIGH

Refund Policy Friction

Willow's default resolution for defective products is an exchange, not a refund. Many customers report being denied refunds even within 30 days of purchase with a defective unit. Agents reportedly escalate to "higher management" with no guarantee of outcome. This is a classic policy gap that creates BBB and social media blowback.

Policy Gap Complaint Escalator Needs Clear Guardrails
Source: BBB Complaint Archive — Multiple verified cases, 2024–2025
MED

Parts Availability & Dry-Before-Use Requirement

Replacement parts — duckbills, flanges, sizing inserts — are frequently out of stock on the website and in stores. Separately, the pump requires all parts to be completely dry before use, which is operationally difficult for moms pumping 6–8x daily. The "fridge hack" (refrigerating parts between sessions) causes suction loss, and there's no reliable fix communicated by support.

Parts OOS User Education Gap Repeat Contact Driver
Source: IBCLC Review — thebreastfeedingmama.com · bemybreastfriend.com (2024)
MED

App Connectivity & Bluetooth Glitches

The Willow app randomly disconnects from the pump, disrupting session tracking and suction control. This is especially frustrating for working moms who rely on the app during office hours. The bot-first support model loops users through FAQs before reaching a human, adding friction when the issue needs live troubleshooting.

Tech Support App Issue Bluetooth / Pairing
Source: IBCLC Review · pine.ai contact analysis (2026) · r/breastfeeding threads
MED

Flange Sizing & Fit Issues

Willow Go ships with only 21mm and 24mm flanges. Most women fall outside these sizes and need to purchase inserts separately — but this isn't always clearly communicated pre-purchase. Lactation consultants note that incorrect flange sizing causes pain, reduced milk output, and nipple damage. Customers contact support thinking the pump is broken when the actual issue is fit.

Sizing Education Repeat Contact Clinical Knowledge Needed
Source: IBCLC Review — thebreastfeedingmama.com (Nov 2025)
MED

Insurance Reimbursement Confusion

ACA requires insurance to cover breast pumps, but Willow is not always in-network. Customers get stuck between Willow's insurance claim process and their insurer's documentation requirements. Willow explicitly warns customers not to submit claims through both channels simultaneously — but the instructions are complex, and errors result in claim denial. This is a high-stakes, low-tolerance contact type.

Insurance Navigation Documentation Errors High Stakes
Source: onewillow.com/pages/insurance · pine.ai contact analysis (2026)
LOW

Price-to-Value Perception

Multiple reviewers and lactation consultants flag that Willow's pricing is harder to justify given increasing competition from Momcozy, Elvie, and others at lower price points. Customers feel they're paying a brand premium but getting inconsistent quality. Not a direct support contact driver, but it shapes incoming sentiment and willingness to accept resolutions short of a refund.

Sentiment Brand Risk Competitive Pressure
Source: bemybreastfriend.com (Dec 2024) · newlittlelife.com (Jan 2025)
