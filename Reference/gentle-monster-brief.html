Client Overview

Fashion Eyewear Meets Experiential Retail

Gentle Monster is a South Korean luxury eyewear brand founded in 2011 under parent company IICOMBINED. Built around "weird beauty" and high-end experimentation, the brand is as famous for its immersive, art-installation-style flagship stores as it is for its frames. Products are priced between $240–$400+ and are designed specifically for Asian facial structures, which gave the brand massive tailwinds in Asian-diaspora and K-pop adjacent markets globally.


The US is their largest market (47% of revenue), with flagship locations in NYC SoHo, LA, and American Dream NJ, and a Toronto store opening in 2026. Active tech pivots include a Google Android XR smart glasses partnership ($100M investment, 2025) and a Samsung Galaxy Glasses collab — moving Gentle Monster from pure fashion into the intelligent eyewear category. Revenue declined slightly in FY2025 — the first dip in five years — making CX quality and retention a more urgent lever than before.

CNX Opportunity

A premium brand with a documented CS gap at scale. Customers paying $300–$400+ are expecting luxury-tier service and getting inconsistent, slow, and policy-rigid responses. This is a solvable problem — one CNX is built for.

Why This Account Matters

🌏 Asian-First Brand DNA

Filipino agents naturally align with the brand's Asian aesthetic sensibility, K-pop cultural references, and customer persona. Cultural IQ reduces ramp time significantly.

📈 Growth Story + Tech Pivot

Google and Samsung partnerships signal intelligent eyewear scale-up. That means more product complexity, more contact volume, and higher-value interactions — all in CNX's sweet spot.

🛒 US-Dominant E-Commerce

47% of revenue from the US, DTC via web + flagships. Shipping, returns, and order queries dominate current complaint channels — classic e-comm CS.

⚠️ CS Reputation Risk

BBB, Trustpilot, and Thingtesting reviews are consistently critical of CS responsiveness. Brand is growing faster than their support structure can handle. Entry point: stabilize and scale.

💼 Premium Segment = Premium CSAT

Customers at this price point are emotionally invested in the brand. High-empathy, brand-aligned agents drive outsized satisfaction scores. CNX at our best.

Six Stages, Three Moments That Matter

01 Discovery

Customer discovers GM via K-pop content, celebrity endorsement, collab drop (Disney F1, Maison Margiela), or TikTok/Instagram. First exposure is typically a specific frame tied to a cultural moment.

📌 Contact trigger: Pre-sale product queries, availability, Asia-fit sizing
😍 Excited / Aspirational
02 Purchase

Online via gentlemonster.com (primary) or in-store at flagship. Payment via card, Klarna. High-consideration purchase — customers research before committing. Prescription lens availability decision point here.

📌 Contact trigger: RX lens compatibility, payment issues, order confirmation delays
✅ Confident / Anticipating
03 Delivery

Order ships via UPS or FedEx. Tracking delays, wrong item received, or damaged product on arrival are documented pain points. Online customers especially vulnerable — no in-store quality check.

📌 Contact trigger: Tracking issues, wrong item, arrival damage, IOR/customs
⏳ Anxious / Impatient
04 Unboxing

First physical product interaction. For many customers, this is a high-emotion moment tied to brand expectation. Frame quality mismatch vs. in-store display, sticker/tag condition, and packaging integrity all factor here.

📌 Contact trigger: Defect claims, quality mismatch, missing parts/tags
😬 Hopeful / Vulnerable
05 Return / Repair

Highest-friction stage. Policy enforcement is inconsistent in-store. Online returns face tag/sticker requirements customers weren't informed about. Repair logistics involve third-party couriers with unresolved ownership gaps.

📌 Contact trigger: Return denials, policy disputes, repair status, refund delays
😤 Frustrated / Betrayed
06 Resolution

Customer either receives satisfactory resolution (refund, replacement, repair) or escalates publicly via BBB, Trustpilot, or social media. Currently, most interactions fall into public escalation rather than quiet resolution.

📌 Contact trigger: Escalation to BBB/review platforms, chargeback initiation
🤝 Relieved (if resolved) / Burned (if not)
Priority Zones

Where CNX Has Highest Impact

🔴 Stage 3 — Delivery Ownership

CNX agents must own delivery issues end-to-end — coordinate with UPS/FedEx, file claims, and drive resolution without bouncing customers to the carrier. No-ownership handoffs are the #1 complaint driver right now.

🔴 Stage 5 — Returns & Policy Mediation

Return and exchange handling needs clear empowerment guidelines. Agents need authority to honor policy for unworn, tagged items — not create unapproved blockers. CNX governance framework applied here = CSAT recovery.

🟡 Stage 6 — Escalation Diversion

Customers who reach BBB or Trustpilot first are customers who weren't reached fast enough. A proactive follow-up queue post-resolution (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7) diverts the public escalation funnel before it starts.

Hiring Criteria

Profile Requirements

✅ Must-Have

  • Strong written English — chat and email
  • Empathy and patience under pressure
  • Familiarity with K-culture, fashion brands
  • E-commerce CS or retail experience preferred
  • High attention to detail (returns/policy adherence)

  • Lens types and RX compatibility knowledge

🚫 Disqualifiers

  • Passive escalation behavior (just logs, doesn't resolve)
